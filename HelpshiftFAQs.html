Payments

What is the start payment?

To keep monthly payments low without locking you in to a long-term contract, every car you get through Fair requires a Start Payment. It's an upfront fee due at signing to drive away. It is not refundable past the 3-day cancellation period.

With Fair, you can keep the car as long as you’d like and return it at any time with 5 days' notice. Every time you get a different car, you will have to pay another start payment.

Does my credit affect my monthly payments?

After you scan your driver's license we'll do a soft credit check, which doesn't impact your credit,  to determine your eligibility.  This allows us to provide you with a tailored range of monthly payments that could work for you.

When is my first payment due?

Unlike a traditional lease, Fair doesn't bill you in advance but at the end of the billing period.

If you're a personal-use customer, your first monthly payment won't be due until 30 days after taking possession of your car. This allows us to prorate your final bill.

If you're a rideshare customer, your first weekly payment won't be due for 7 days from pickup, with your pickup date counting as the first billing day.

How are estimated monthly payments different than my actual pricing?

Our website's Preview Mode shows estimated monthly payments. Once you create an account and  pre-qualify, we'll have the information we need to provide your personalized pricing and the cars in your offer range.

How is registration included in my payments?

Your Fair Vehicle will be registered in your name. Unlike traditional leases and loans, you don't have to pay out of pocket for registration. Fair cars come with registration for as long as you drive the car.

Can I lower my monthly or weekly payment?

A major benefit of Fair is that our pricing is negotiation-free. We encourage you to stay within your budget, but we're unable to modify the payments any further.

Why do I need to use my bank login?

You can link your bank account to quickly and easily set up payments. We may also use it when you pre-qualify as a tool to verify your identity and confirm affordability.

I'm having trouble making the start payment. Why am I getting an error?

We'd be happy to help. If you're getting an error message, please call your bank directly and check the following:

  • The amount of funds accessible for immediate use.
  • Often, the amount showing in the account does not reflect what is available for immediate use, as transactions or deposits may be pending.
  • Fair is authorized on the account to withdraw this amount.
  • Whether their card is being declined for any other reason.

If you've called your bank on these items and still need further assistance, please don't hesitate to chat with us in the app so we can further assist. We're here and happy to assist!

How do I change my payment method for autopay?

To change your payment method, tap the Main Menu and select payments. Just add the new card and authorize and you'll be switched over.

How do you determine my monthly qualification amount?

Fair has its own unique process to determine how much you pre-qualify for. You can start the process by scanning your license. Simply pre-qualifying won't affect your credit score. Once you’ve completed this process, it’ll only take a few moments to find out your maximum monthly payment.

How much is the late charge?

You may incur a late charge for any payment that is not received 10 days after your due date. That amount will be the lower of $25 or 5% of the amount due.

What if I can't afford my car anymore?

If you can no longer afford your car, you can simply return it anytime. We do just ask that you give us five days notice in order to for us to set the return pickup. Please remember, you'll still be due a final, prorated bill, as payments are due at the end of the period.

My bank isn't listed when I try to connect an account. What else can I use?

We apologize for any inconvenience. If your bank isn't listed, you can authorize your payments using a credit card. Personal-use customers may also use a debit card.

Am I eligible for the leasing tax incentives?

We encourage you to consult a CPA to confirm eligibility.

Am I required to set up automatic payments?

Fair requires you to set up a form of digital automatic payments.  For all customers, you may use your bank account or credit card. For personal-use customers, you may also use a debit card.

Returns

I just got my car but I don't love it. Can I return it?

Fair's 3-Day No Risk Return policy gives you up to 3 days and 100 miles after signing to bring your car back for a full refund of your Start Payment, as long as the vehicle is in good condition. Beyond 3 days or 100 miles, your Start Payment becomes non-refundable but you may return at any time with 5 days' notice. Not available in all states.

Something to note, you'll only be able to cancel your agreement for a full refund once every 12 months.

Massachusetts residents: There is no 3-day cancellation in Massachusetts, but you'll be able to see and test the vehicle within 3 days of checkout. If you don't love your Fair Vehicle, you can cancel for a full refund before accepting the vehicle.

How does billing work if I return in the middle of a billing period?

After returning your car, your final payment is prorated to reflect the number of days you've had the car since the last billing cycle closed. As a reminder, Fair bills in arrears, or at the end of the period, so you'll be due a final bill.  

I just returned my car. Why did I get another bill?

Remember, Fair doesn't bill in advance, but payments are due at the end of each period. At return, you'll still need to make a final, prorated payment.

If you believe your bill is incorrect, please call our billing division at 866-443-3288 to review this final bill.

If I change Fair cars, will that impact my credit?

We encourage our customers to think of Fair as a better alternative when compared to a traditional lease or a loan. With Fair, there are no fixed terms, so returning your car doesn’t negatively impact your credit.

At this time, Fair only allows a customer to get one vehicle at a time.  So to get another vehicle with Fair you will need return your current Fair vehicle and go through the App again to place another order. Please note changes to your financial history may affect your personalized pricing.

I'm done with my Fair car. How do I return it?

If you're ready to return, just chat with us and provide the following:

  • 3 pickup dates, Monday through Friday, and windows of time you're available at least 3 business days from today.
  • Please select one of these windows, 10 a.m. - 2 p.m. and/or 5 p.m. - 9 p.m., for each date.
  • Pickup address during these windows. We can even pick up from your work!
  • Any special pickup instructions.

Please note, we're not able to accommodate requests sooner than 3 business days from now. Something to keep in mind, you must accompany the vehicle at return.

As recurring payments are due at the end of the billing period, you'll be charged your final, prorated bill for the time you've kept the car in that billing cycle as well as any applicable excess mileage or wear and tear charges. Please note, it may take additional time to update billing with final amounts.

We work hard to make this as smooth and easy for you as possible and appreciate your assistance in getting this ready for you.

*In Massachusetts, please have your registration and license plate numbers available. We will need this in order to notify the DMV of your return, which will allow you to stop your insurance policy on the vehicle.

In the event you need to cancel your return, please provide Fair 48 hours-notice. When you cancel your return, the required 5 days' notice will restart.

Vehicles

What does CPO mean?

CPO stands for certified pre-owned. Each vehicle manufacturer has a different standard for a vehicle to qualify as a CPO. Generally, CPO vehicles undergo a detailed, multi-point inspection and reconditioning process and often include an extended warranty.

Do I have to maintain my car?

Yes, you are responsible for properly maintaining your car. But with routine maintenance included, oil and filter changes, tire rotations and multi-point inspections are covered.

Can I test drive?

Our program comes with a 3-day, 100-mile cancellation policy that begins when you sign your agreement. This way you can test the car with no risk. Not available in all states.

Massachusetts residents: There is no 3-day cancellation in Massachusetts, but you'll be able to see and test drive the vehicle within 3 days of checkout. If you don't love your Fair Vehicle, you can cancel for a full refund before accepting the vehicle.

Understanding base mileage

Most Fair vehicles come with a standard base mileage of 10,000 miles. If you expect to be driving more, you can adjust the mileage to fit your needs.

Note: Some of our more expensive cars (>$50,000) may start with less than 10k miles.

Adding additional miles after purchase

You cannot add additional mileage after purchase. We encourage you to add extra miles upfront, as Fair will discount the extra miles purchased, and we’ll refund any unused extra miles you purchased upon return.  Please note, if you drive less than the base mileage (eg. 10,000 miles), we do not refund for mileage below that point.

Do you check my credit?

When you scan your driver’s license during signup and provide authorization, Fair  initiates a soft credit check with Transunion.  This is also known as a soft inquiry, and does not affect your credit score.  A soft credit inquiry allows us to personalize our decisioning so that you can shop on real pricing.

 

When you decide on a vehicle that you want, you will start the checkout process.   After we confirm the vehicle and when you are ready to finalize and sign our contract, we will do a hard credit check as happens for any automobile transaction.  This is required by the credit bureaus and confirms you are the individual with that history and that nothing has changed significantly since you were pre-qualified.

Mileage

Do all the personal-use vehicles start with 10,000 miles?

That is the standard base mileage for most Fair vehicles, although that number can vary.  The good news is that you can add additional miles to fit your needs.

Why do I have to choose extra miles before checkout?

For personal-use vehicles, Fair provides a discount when you purchase extra miles up front. We’ll even reimburse you at return for any extra miles purchased that you don’t use.  These extra miles cannot be added after checkout.

If you are over the miles at return, you will be charged the standard mileage fee outlined in your Fair agreement.

Please note, if you drive less than the base mileage (eg. 10,000 miles), we do not refund for mileage below that point.

How is the mileage allowance calculated?

The miles per year are prorated and calculated for the time you possess the Fair vehicle. To get your total mileage limit, take your miles allowed per year, divide it by 365, and then multiply by the total number of days you will possess the Fair vehicle.

Excessive Wear and Tear

What is considered excess wear & tear?

Some examples of excess wear include dents, cracks, scratches and damaged upholstery. In the sample agreement in the app, you can see further details on what might be considered excess wear and tear.

Minor dings, dents, and glass chips (not cracks) are normal and are not considered to be excess wear under Fair's program.

Fair does offer excess wear and tear protection at checkout, which will cover certain excess wear charges upon return. Please see your Fair contract at checkout for a list of exclusions.

Are there limits to the excess wear and tear coverage?

There are limits and exclusions on what’s covered. You can preview the Excess Wear & Tear Protection Waiver in our sample agreements, under "See it All Upfront." Waiver limits depend on the vehicle and will be provided at checkout.

Warranty

What is the difference between the Limited Warranty and Powertrain Warranty?

The Limited Warranty covers most mechanical repairs. Details on any exclusions can be found in your Fair contract.


For some vehicles, there is only a Limited Powertrain Warranty, which covers the engine, transmission, power steering, and cooling system. You can see your Fair contract for more details on any exclusions.

What is covered under the Fair Limited Warranty?

The Fair Limited Warranty covers most major mechanical components of the Fair Vehicle, with some exclusions. To find a list of what's not covered, you may visit the Fair Warranty Agreement.

If you have a Fair car and are experiencing mechanical issues, please call our warranty administrator at (888) 817-4627.

How do I know if my Fair car is still under manufacturer's warranty?

Please check your manufacturer's website for warranty provisions on your vehicle. This will depend on the year and mileage.

The great thing about Fair is that your car will be covered with a limited warranty as well. Once the manufacturer's warranty goes away, Fair’s limited warranty kicks in. For details about your specific limited warranty, review the Fair contract.

How do I use my warranty benefit?

Our warranty administrator can assist you with Fair's limited warranty. Please call them at 888-817-4627, Monday - Friday 6 a.m. to 2 p.m. PST.

If the claim is covered under the limited warranty, they will find you an approved service facility who can assist with the work. Please note, there may be $100 deductible on eligible claims. We encourage you to review your Fair limited warranty document for more information on how to make a claim and coverage.
